Survivor Actions

1. Chest Prop

Rummaging through a chest allows the player to remove a token and draw a card from the Item Deck. Items have a name, use condition, effect, and a number of copies in the deck. A Survivor can hold only one item at a time.

2. Locker Prop

Hide in a locker to be hidden from interaction and considered off the board for most game effects. Exception: if the Killer opens the locker.

3. Crow Prop

Allows the Survivor to take a bonus turn at the cost of giving the Killer one Blood Point from the supply. On the bonus turn, the Survivor removes one Movement Card to move along the corresponding path. They may interact again after moving. Survivors can activate multiple bonus turns per round, but the Killer may only have one bonus turn per round.

4. Pallet Prop

Allows the Survivor to place the pallet onto a path to block it.

5. Generator Prop

The player performs a Skill Check (dice roll resulting in 1–5 or a failure). Any number except 5 = success; 5 = great success; failure = no success. Success: add 1 progress token. Great success: add 2 progress tokens. Failure: no tokens added. Once a generator has 3 progress tokens, it is considered complete and moved to the Progress Bar. Any failure gives the Killer 1 Blood Point.

6. Gate Prop

Once all four generators are complete, the gates are powered. Survivors can perform a Skill Check to add a progress token to the gate. Once the gate reaches 3 progress tokens, it opens and Survivors win the game.

7. Hook Prop

Survivors can sabotage by placing an Entity token on the hook for one round. If a Survivor is rescued while on a sabotaged hook, the active player gains 1 Blood Point. Both Survivors may move along any path once without a movement card.

8. Totem Prop

Survivors perform a Skill Check to remove the totem, gaining 2 Blood Points.

9. Heal Action

Requires no token or prop, but another wounded Survivor must be in the same space. The active Survivor rolls a Skill Check. Success removes the wound from the other figure.
